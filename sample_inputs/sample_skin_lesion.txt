SURGICAL PATHOLOGY REPORT

Specimen: Skin, right forearm, shave biopsy.

Clinical history: 61-year-old male with a slowly enlarging pearly papule on the right forearm.

Microscopic description: Sections show nests of basaloid cells extending from the epidermis into the superficial dermis with peripheral palisading and retraction artifact, consistent with basal cell carcinoma, nodular type. The deep margin is positive; the lesion extends to the base of the specimen. There is mild chronic inflammation in the surrounding dermis. No perineural invasion is identified.

Diagnosis: Skin, right forearm, shave biopsy - basal cell carcinoma, nodular type, present at the deep margin.

Comment: Complete excision or other definitive treatment is recommended given the positive deep margin.
